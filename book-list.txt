Certainly, here are some of the best Golang programming books for real-life project development:

"The Go Programming Language" by Alan A. A. Donovan and Brian W. Kernighan - This book is considered the definitive guide to Golang and covers all aspects of the language, including its design, syntax, and use in real-world projects.

"Go in Action" by William Kennedy, Brian Ketelsen, and Erik St. Martin - This book is focused on practical examples of Golang usage and covers topics such as building RESTful web services, concurrency, and testing.

"Concurrency in Go" by Katherine Cox-Buday - This book is specifically focused on Golang's concurrency features, which are essential for building scalable and efficient applications.

"Web Development with Go: Building Scalable Web Apps and RESTful Services" by Shiju Varghese - This book covers web development with Golang and includes examples of building RESTful services, handling user authentication, and working with databases.

"Go Systems Programming: Master Linux and Unix system-level programming with Go" by Mihalis Tsoukalos - This book focuses on Golang's system-level programming capabilities and includes examples of building Linux and Unix applications.

All of these books are highly recommended by Golang developers and cover a range of topics that are essential for real-life project development. Choose the one that best fits your needs and interests, and happy reading!
